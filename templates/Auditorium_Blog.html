AUDITORIUM INFORMATION

TSC Auditorium

The Teacher-Student Centre or TSC of the University of Dhaka is a building on the Dhaka University campus in Shahbagh, Dhaka, Bangladesh. The Centre was established in 1961 by the Dhaka University as the Division publicAffairs. Across the green lawn to the east of the Teacher-Student Centre stands a small Greek Mausoleum. It was constructed in 1915.

RC Majumdar Arts Auditorium

An auditorium at the Lecture Theatre (near Kalabhabon) on the Dhaka University campus has been named after its former Vice Chancellor and noted historian Dr Romesh Chandra (RC) Majumdar. Vice Chancellor Prof SMA Faiz inaugurated the auditorium on Monday as the chief guest at a function to mark the occasion. The air-conditioned auditorium will be available on rent for seminars, symposiums and educational discourses under appropriate permission from the authorities.

Nawab Ali Chowdhury Senate Bhaban

Nabab Nawab Ali Chowdhury Senate Bhaban, University of Dhaka was completed in the year 2007 and it is a part of the University of Dhaka. It was named after one of the founding fathers.Nabab Nawab Ali Chowdhury Senate Bhaban, University of Dhaka has a 500 seated auditorium provided with digital conference auto system, two committee rooms and three well furnished lobbies, situated at the level 3.

Lecture Theater, Fine Arts

The Faculty of Fine Arts (FFA) was established in 1948 as the Dhaka Art School. It was the first art school in the region and became the main centre of art and cultural practice. Since 1956 it has been situated in Shahbag, Dhaka, close to the Bangladesh National Museum. Architect Muzharul Islam designed the building. In 2008, the institute took its current name when it became one of the faculties of the University of Dhaka.
